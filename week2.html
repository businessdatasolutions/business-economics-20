Week 2: Marktvormen

Introductie tot de Onderwerpen:

This week delves into how markets are structured and how firms behave within them. We will compare competitive markets with those where firms have more power, and discuss factors like pricing, consumer behaviour, and government influence.

Relevante E-book Units:

  • Unit 7: The Firm and its Customers focuses on firms producing differentiated products. It explains how these firms make decisions about price and quantity to maximize profits, taking into account the demand curve and cost structure. Concepts covered include demand curves, isoprofit curves, marginal cost, and the elasticity of demand. This unit also touches upon firm decisions regarding production, hiring, innovation, advertising, and influencing policy. (Open PDF)
  • Unit 8: Supply and Demand: Price-Taking and Competitive Markets contrasts with Unit 7 by examining markets where both buyers and sellers are price-takers. We will learn how the interaction of supply and demand determines a market equilibrium. Key concepts include willingness to pay (for demand) and willingness to accept/reservation price (for supply). We will analyze how supply and demand shocks affect equilibrium prices and quantities. (Open PDF)
  • Unit 11: Rent-Seeking, Price-Setting, and Market Dynamics discusses how markets work, sometimes leading to equilibrium and sometimes not. It highlights the role of prices as messages and introduces the concept of market equilibration through rent-seeking when competitive markets are not in equilibrium. Concepts like price bubbles are also mentioned. (Open PDF)
  • Unit 12: Markets, Efficiency, and Public Policy introduces the concept of market failure. While not focused on market structure itself, it explains situations where market outcomes are inefficient, which can relate to the behaviour of firms and consumers within different structures. (Open PDF)

Leerdoelen:

  • Understand different market structures, including competitive markets and markets with differentiated products.
  • Analyze the structure, behaviour, and performance characteristics associated with different market forms.
  • Explain how prices and quantities are determined in competitive markets through supply and demand.
  • Explain how firms producing differentiated products choose prices and quantities to maximize profits.
  • Understand consumer behaviour, including willingness to pay and price sensitivity.
  • Discuss the role and potential impact of government regulation.
  • Introduce the concepts of B2B and B2C interactions in economic analysis.
